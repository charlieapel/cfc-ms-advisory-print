Amusement Park Challenge

Relationship-Building Activity

Preparation

  • Divide the class into groups of 3â€“5 students.
  • Allow at least 15 minutes for this activity. If time runs out, continue when time allows.
Print this Activity

Materials

  • Lined Paper
  • Writing Utensil
  • Groups of 3–5

Overview

Explore ways that people deliberately cause others to feel emotions

Introduction

Today, your challenge will be to design rides for a new amusement park. The park is called Emotion World, and each ride should be designed to make the people who ride it experience a specific emotion.

Steps

  1. Each group is assigned a different emotion.

    Additional information

    Examples of emotions: surprise, joy, boredom, fear, excitement, wonder, confusion, or happiness.

  2. Design an amusement park ride that will make the riders feel the assigned emotion. Write a description of your ride and draw a picture of it.
  3. Show and describe your Emotional Theme Park attraction to the class.

Reflection

Quietly reflect on the following question

What are some things people do in the real world to deliberately cause you to feel a particular emotion?

Additional information

  • Have students reflect quietly on the prompt.
  • Call on students to tell the class their ideas, as time allows.
